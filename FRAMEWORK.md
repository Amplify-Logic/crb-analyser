# CRB Analyser - Framework & Methodology

> This document defines the analytical framework used by the CRB Agent to generate reports.
> All recommendations must adhere to these principles.

---

## Core Philosophy

### The Two Pillars (Both Required)

Every recommendation must score positively on BOTH pillars:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                             │
│   CUSTOMER VALUE              BUSINESS HEALTH               │
│   "Does this make their       "Does this help the           │
│    customers' lives better?"   business survive & thrive?"  │
│                                                             │
│   - Better experience          - Sustainable margins        │
│   - More value delivered       - Build capability           │
│   - Increased trust/loyalty    - Reduce existential risk    │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Rejection criteria:**
- High customer value + hurts business → "Unsustainable" (don't recommend)
- High business value + hurts customers → "Short-sighted" (don't recommend)
- Only recommend when BOTH benefit

## Value Calculation Methodology

### Two Types of Value

**1. VALUE SAVED (Efficiency)**
```
Time saved per week × Hourly cost × 52 weeks = Annual savings

Example:
- AI automates reporting: 10 hours/week saved
- Employee cost: €50/hour (fully loaded)
- Annual savings: 10 × €50 × 52 = €26,000
```

**2. VALUE CREATED (Growth)**
```
New revenue opportunities enabled by AI/automation

Examples:
- Increased conversion rate: +2% × €500K revenue = €10,000
- Higher customer LTV: +15% retention × €200 avg value = €X
- New service offerings: AI-powered service @ €500/client × 20 clients = €10,000
- Faster time-to-market: 2 weeks earlier × €5K/week opportunity cost = €10,000
```

## CRB Detail Structure

### COST Analysis

| Category | Short Term (0-6mo) | Mid Term (6-18mo) | Long Term (18mo+) |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Software/Licensing** | Initial subscription | Annual renewal | Multi-year TCO |
| **Implementation** | Setup, integration | Customization | Upgrades |
| **Training** | Initial onboarding | Advanced training | Ongoing learning |
| **Opportunity Cost** | Team time diverted | Productivity dip | - |

**Evidence requirements:**
- Vendor pricing (verified, dated)
- Industry implementation benchmarks
- Case study cost data

### RISK Analysis

| Category | Short Term | Mid Term | Long Term |
|----------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Technical** | Integration failures | Scaling issues | Technical debt |
| **Adoption** | Team resistance | Partial adoption | Abandonment |
| **Vendor** | Support quality | Price increases | Vendor viability |
| **Market** | - | Competitor response | Technology obsolescence |

**Evidence requirements:**
- Peer-reviewed studies on AI/automation adoption
- Vendor stability indicators (funding, customer base)
- Industry failure rate data
- Case studies of failed implementations

**Risk scoring:**
- **Low**: <20% probability, <€5K impact
- **Medium**: 20-50% probability, €5K-25K impact
- **High**: >50% probability, >€25K impact

### BENEFIT Analysis

| Category | Short Term | Mid Term | Long Term |
|----------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Efficiency** | Hours saved | Processes automated | Full optimization |
| **Quality** | Error reduction | Consistency | Excellence |
| **Growth** | Quick wins | New capabilities | Market leadership |
| **Strategic** | - | Competitive parity | Competitive advantage |

**Benefit types to always consider:**
1. Time savings → converted to € value
2. Cost reduction → direct savings
3. Revenue increase → growth opportunities
4. Risk reduction → avoided costs
5. Customer satisfaction → retention value
6. Employee satisfaction → reduced turnover
7. Strategic positioning → hard to quantify but note it

## The Three Options Pattern

Every recommendation MUST include three options:

### Option A: Off-the-Shelf
- **What**: Existing SaaS, minimal customization
- **When**: Fast implementation needed, standard use case
- **Pros**: Quick, low risk, proven
- **Cons**: Limited differentiation, ongoing fees

### Option B: Best-in-Class
- **What**: Premium vendor, full implementation
- **When**: Serious investment, need full capabilities
- **Pros**: Comprehensive, supported, scalable
- **Cons**: Higher cost, longer implementation

### Option C: Custom AI Solution
- **What**: Built specifically for them
- **When**: Unique needs, want competitive moat
- **Pros**: Tailored, owned, differentiating
- **Cons**: Higher upfront cost, needs maintenance

**Why always include Custom:**
- AI development costs dropping 50%+ year over year
- Can become competitive advantage
- Opens minds to possibilities
- We offer this as services tier (€2K+)

## Quality Standards

### Evidence Requirements

**For Costs:**
- Primary: Vendor pricing pages (with date verified)
- Secondary: Industry reports (Gartner, Forrester, etc.)
- Tertiary: Case studies with disclosed costs

**For Risks:**
- Primary: Peer-reviewed studies
- Secondary: Industry analyst reports
- Tertiary: Documented case studies of failures

**For Benefits:**
- Primary: Peer-reviewed ROI studies
- Secondary: Vendor case studies (with skepticism discount)
- Tertiary: Industry benchmarks

### Confidence Levels

| Level | Criteria | Display |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **High** | Multiple reliable sources, direct evidence | Green indicator |
| **Medium** | Single reliable source or inferred | Yellow indicator |
| **Low** | Estimated, limited data | Gray indicator |

### Transparency Rules

1. **Always show assumptions** - Every calculation must list assumptions
2. **Always cite sources** - Every claim needs a source
3. **Always show ranges** - Never single-point estimates, always min-max
4. **Always note limitations** - What we don't know
5. **Always date data** - When was pricing/benchmark collected

## Impartiality & Honesty

### Core Principle

**We are a TRUSTED ADVISOR, not a salesperson.**

Our job is to give the BEST answer for this specific business, even when that answer is uncomfortable or means recommending nothing.

### Be Willing to Say

| Honest Answer | When to Say It |
|---------------|----------------|
| "You're not ready for AI yet" | Fundamentals not in place (data, processes, team) |
| "This doesn't need automation" | Process works fine, human touch is the value |
| "The ROI doesn't justify it" | Costs outweigh benefits for their scale |
| "Your team is too small" | Overhead would exceed benefit |
| "Wait 6-12 months" | Tools aren't mature enough for their use case |
| "The cheap option is better" | Premium features won't be used |
| "Don't automate this" | Human touch IS their competitive advantage |

### Vendor Impartiality

- **Never favor a vendor without clear justification**
- If free/cheap works, recommend it over expensive
- Disclose vendors known for aggressive sales tactics
- Always consider lock-in risks
- "Build vs buy" sometimes = "neither"

### Honest Assessments

- If data is insufficient → say so, don't fabricate confidence
- If bigger problems exist than AI can solve → name them
- If competitors are too far ahead → be realistic
- If solopreneur will be overwhelmed → warn them

### Why This Matters

**Our value is TRUST.**

A report that saves someone from a bad €10K investment is worth more than a report that sounds impressive but leads them astray.

The best outcome is a business that thrives. Sometimes that means NOT implementing AI.

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Recommend)

### Red Flags

| Pattern | Why It's Bad | What to Do Instead |
|---------|--------------|---------------------|
| "Replace team with AI" | Hurts customers long-term, destroys capability | "Augment team with AI" |
| "Cheapest option" | Usually false economy | "Best value option" |
| "Everyone's doing it" | Not evidence-based | "Here's the evidence" |
| "AI can do everything" | Overpromising | "AI is good at X, humans at Y" |
| Cost-cutting only | Ignores growth | Balance efficiency + growth |
| Vendor-locked solution | High switching cost risk | Consider portability |

### Questions to Always Ask

1. "What happens to customer experience?"
2. "What happens if this fails?"
3. "What happens in 3 years?"
4. "What are we NOT considering?"
5. "Is this sustainable?"
